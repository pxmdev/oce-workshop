Other FTA Funding Opportunities for Tribes

Hyperlinks referenced on Slides 11 and 12 of the FTA TTP Presentation (04.20.2026), with a concise description of each program and the governing section of U.S. Code.

SLIDE 11 Formula Programs
Rural Areas Formula Program 49 U.S.C. § 5311
Formula

Formula funding apportioned to states for public transportation in areas with fewer than 50,000 people. Supports planning, capital, operating, and administrative costs for rural transit providers. Tribes typically participate as sub-recipients of their state DOT's 5311 program, though the separate Tribal Transit Program (Section 5311(c)) is a take-down from this same pot and flows directly to tribes.

transit.dot.gov/rural-formula-grants-5311
SLIDE 12 Competitive & Discretionary Programs
Bus and Bus Facilities Competitive Program 49 U.S.C. § 5339(b)
Competitive

Discretionary, nationally competitive grant for capital projects to replace, rehabilitate, and purchase buses and related equipment, and to construct bus-related facilities (maintenance, storage, transfer). Awarded through periodic Notices of Funding Opportunity (NOFOs); tribes are eligible direct applicants. Funds can be combined with other FTA formula awards on the same project.

transit.dot.gov/bus-program
Low or No Emission (Low-No) Program 49 U.S.C. § 5339(c)
Competitive

Competitive grant program funding the purchase or lease of zero-emission and low-emission transit buses, along with supporting facilities and infrastructure (charging, fueling, maintenance upgrades). Eligible technologies include battery-electric, hydrogen fuel cell, and hybrid. A required share of each award (currently 5%) must go to workforce development and training.
